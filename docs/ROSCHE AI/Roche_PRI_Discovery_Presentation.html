Technology · The Engine

Claude on Vertex AI, inside Roche's own Google Cloud.

One specialised discovery agent on a frontier reasoning engine, grounded in a versioned rulebook, inside the perimeter Roche already operates, secures and audits. Not a SaaS subscription, not a new data processor, no new procurement path.

Grounded, not creative

The agent may only flag what it can cite.

Cases, the PRI decision rules and product documentation are the only sources. Every flag carries written reasoning with references a reviewer, an auditor or an inspector can verify. It says "not enough information" instead of inventing an answer.

Frozen, testable, auditable

Version-pinned model, golden-dataset gates.

Validated against one pinned Claude version: same case, same rules, same answer. Upgrades only through change control with regression on the golden dataset of already-judged cases. A validated system cannot sit on a model that changes under it.

Inside the perimeter

The model comes to the data.

EU region, VPC Service Controls, CMEK, IAM and audit logging on every call. Zero retention, zero training on Roche data, contractually. Billed through the existing Google agreement. No data path to the public internet or to the model vendor.

Technology · What The Model Itself Costs

Cents per case. Billed at cost, no markup.

Three Claude tiers are available on Vertex AI. Which one reads which case is an evidence decision, made at the Stage 1 gate: the cheapest model that clears the accuracy targets on the golden dataset wins.

Claude Haiku · the sieve (optional)
≈ $1 / $5 per M tokens in / out

A cheap pre-screen for cases with obviously no safety signal. Used only if Stage 1 proves it misses nothing. In a "never miss a PRI" context, the sieve must earn its place with evidence, not savings.

Claude Sonnet · the workhorse
≈ $3 / $15 per M tokens in / out

Reads every case against the rulebook: case file plus relevant rules in one pass, reasoning and citations out. With the rulebook prompt-cached, a full case review costs €0.03–0.10.

Claude Opus · the second opinion
≈ $5 / $25 per M tokens in / out

Escalation for the ambiguous 5–10%: borderline phrasings, conflicting details, rare case types. Deeper reasoning where it matters, adding cents to the average case, not euros.

Appendix · Inside The MVP Price

The model reads the cases in a night.
The money buys the proof that it read them right.

Stage 0 + Stage 1, €675–775K, ~7 months. The AI run itself is a rounding error. What the budget actually builds:

~20% · The rulebook

Decision logic extracted and versioned.

What makes a case a PRI today lives across SOPs, training material and the specialists' judgement. Turning it into explicit, machine-readable, version-controlled rules is structured expert work, and it becomes a Roche asset regardless of the tool.

~20% · The measuring stick

Golden dataset, adjudicated.

Hundreds of already-judged cases assembled into a reference set. Where specialists disagree with each other, and on borderline cases they do, the disagreement is resolved and documented. Without this, any accuracy claim is just an opinion.

~30% · The engineering

Pipeline, eval harness, review workspace.

Read-only ingestion from the case system, the harness that measures every change against the golden dataset, and the workspace where specialists confirm or reject flags with one click, every decision logged.

~15% · The iteration

10–20 measured improvement cycles.

The first run always over-flags. Each cycle, a human reads hundreds of model-vs-specialist disagreements, classifies why, refines the rules, re-measures. This reading is where detection quality is actually made.

~10% · The evidence

A result QA can attack and not break.

Methodology, accuracy statistics, worked examples, limitations, reproducibility, plus the traceability groundwork that keeps Stage 3 validation from starting over. "It looks good" is worthless in a QMS; signed evidence is the deliverable.

~5% · The coordination

Access, approvals, gates.

Data access, IAM and Vertex provisioning driven from week 1, the InfoSec checkpoint, and the signed acceptance process. Fixed price means schedule risk on approvals sits with Redsparq, not with Roche's budget.

Knowledge-cell hours are aligned with the existing account: nothing is billed twice, and today's review coverage does not drop while the tool is built. Compare: a large integrator quotes €1–2M for this stage, without the team that already knows the cases.
