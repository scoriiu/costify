9 · Glossary, in Plain Language

No prior technical or AI knowledge assumed. These are the terms used in this document and in the stage gates.

TermWhat it actually means
Certified value baselineToday, nobody has an agreed number for what the current review costs and what the unchecked cases are worth. In Stage 0 we measure it from Roche's own data, and Finance signs off on how it is counted. "Certified" means: a number your own Finance agreed on, so the value discussion never has to reopen.
Rulebook (extraction)The written decision logic: what makes a case a complaint or a reportable incident, for each portfolio. Today it lives in procedures and in experienced people's heads. We turn it into one explicit, versioned document that the AI must follow, and must cite whenever it flags something.
Golden datasetAn answer key. A few hundred past cases that your specialists already judged, assembled so the tool can be graded: it reads them without seeing the answers, and we compare its conclusions with the specialists'. Without this, "the tool works" is an opinion. With it, it is a measured score.
AdjudicationBorderline cases exist where even two experienced specialists would judge differently. Adjudication is deciding, case by case, what the official right answer is, and writing down why. The written tie-breaks also improve the rulebook itself.
Pipeline (read-only)The plumbing that copies case text out of Salesforce and Rexis so the AI can read it. Read-only means exactly that: it can never change, write to, or delete anything in your systems.
Eval harnessAn automatic grading machine. Every time we adjust the tool, it re-reads the whole answer key and produces a fresh score. This guarantees every change is measured, never guessed.
Iteration cycleOne round of: run the tool on the answer key, study every case where it disagrees with your specialists, improve the rules, measure again. Expect 10 to 20 rounds in Stage 1. This is where detection quality is actually made.
Review workspaceThe screen where your specialists see each flagged case together with the tool's written reasoning, and click confirm or reject. Every decision is recorded automatically.
Precision / recallThe two accuracy scores used at the gates. Precision: of everything the tool flagged, how much was truly a problem (few false alarms). Recall: of all the real problems, how many the tool caught (few misses).
Acceptance evidenceThe documented proof, defined and signed before a stage starts, that the stage delivered what was promised, for example the measured score on the answer key. Roche signs acceptance; only then is the stage invoiced.
Stage gateThe formal checkpoint at the end of each stage: Roche reviews the evidence, signs or does not sign acceptance, and decides whether the next stage starts. Exit is possible at every gate.
GxP classificationThe formal decision on how strictly the tool must be validated under medical device quality rules. It is decided in Stage 0, so the validation effort and cost are known before any major commitment.
Validation dossier (GAMP 5, IQ/OQ/PQ)The evidence pack an auditor or regulator expects for software used inside a quality process: written requirements, test protocols, and signed proof that each test passed. GAMP 5 is the pharmaceutical industry's standard method for producing exactly that.
Claude on Vertex AIClaude is the AI reading engine (built by Anthropic). Vertex AI is the Google Cloud service that runs it inside Roche's own cloud environment. Case data never leaves Roche's perimeter, is never sent to the AI vendor, and is never used to train anyone's AI.
Pinned modelThe AI is frozen at one exact version: the same case with the same rules gives the same answer, every time. Upgrades happen only through change control, and only after re-testing on the answer key.
PromptThe written instructions the AI receives with every case: its role, the rules to apply, the format of the answer. Versioned and controlled like a quality document.
Knowledge cellThe small, named group drawn from the 15 specialists. They provide the decision rules, build the answer key, and judge the tool at every gate.
HypercareThe first three months after go-live, with intensified support: fastest response times, close monitoring, immediate fixes.
At cost, quarterly true-upAI computing usage is passed through at the cloud provider's price, with no markup. Every quarter, actual usage is compared to the estimate and the difference is settled, in either direction.
AnonymisationRemoving or masking personal details (names, contact data) from historical cases before analysis, wherever legal and privacy rules require it.
