How It Gets Built

Design once. Repeat 25 times.

The value is not the software. The software exists and Roche pays for it already. The value is validated content built fast, at consistent quality, by the right mix of seniority.

1 · The architect

One senior Salesforce / OmniStudio consultant, 8 months.

Designs the data model, the workflow templates and the integration logic once. Stays on to develop and improve the tool while content is built. Hands over a documented, easy-to-maintain structure.

2 · The builders

Two process editors, 12 months, across the whole catalog.

Copy the templates product by product: pictures, steps, checks. Where workload allows they come from the team already on the Roche account, so they know the products from day one.

3 · The gatekeepers

SMEs approve every workflow before release.

Defined review, version history, audit trail. In a medical products organisation this guidance is quality-relevant documentation and is managed with controlled-document discipline.

The specialist designs everything once. The editors do the high-volume work at a lower cost. That is why ~300 validated workflows fit into €290–330K, not into a seven-figure integration program.

Where AI Works · Inside The Delivery

AI does the heavy lifting. People do the approving.

The content already exists as manuals, work instructions and imagery. Turning it into ~300 structured workflows is exactly the kind of work AI accelerates, and exactly the kind of work it must never sign off.

Drafting

From manual to workflow draft.

AI reads the existing manuals and work instructions and drafts each workflow in the template structure: steps, branching questions, picture placement. The editor corrects and completes instead of starting from a blank page. This is how two editors cover ~300 workflows in twelve months.

Review prep

Every draft pre-checked before the SME sees it.

AI compares each draft against the content model and the source documents, flags gaps and inconsistencies. SMEs spend their 2–4 hours per week judging, not hunting typos. The ≤5-day review turnaround holds, and the timeline holds with it.

Languages

Additional languages at translation cost, not authoring cost.

Phase 1 is authored and validated in English. When more languages come, AI translates the validated content and an SME re-validates per language. The per-language price drops to review effort instead of a rebuild.

Maintenance

Content that stays current instead of decaying.

Salesforce ships three releases a year and products evolve. AI-assisted impact assessment shows which workflows a change touches, so the maintenance retainer stays at the low end of €43–70K and revalidation is targeted, not blanket.

AI is not a line item in this offer. It is the reason ~300 validated workflows, in 13 months, at this price, is a realistic plan instead of an optimistic one.
