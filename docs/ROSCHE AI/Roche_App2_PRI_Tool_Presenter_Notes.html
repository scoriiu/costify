The PRI Tool for Roche

Everything you need to present the 14 slides with confidence. No technical background needed. Plan for about 20 minutes of talking plus questions.

Before you start

  • Open Roche_App2_PRI_Tool_Presentation.html in a browser, press F11 for full screen.
  • Move forward with →, Space or a click. Go back with ← or a click on the left edge of the screen. Home jumps to the first slide, End to the last.
  • Speak slowly. Each slide is built so the picture does most of the work. Your job is to say the point, not to read the slide.
  • If you do not know an answer, say: "Good question, I will get you a precise answer from our delivery team this week." Never improvise numbers.
  • The audience decides about money. They care about three things: risk, cost, and who is accountable. Every answer should land on one of those.

"And at the end, the quality team can only review 20 to 25 percent of cases. Look at the bottom of the slide: 75 to 80 percent are never checked. That is where missed incidents live."

04 The fix: one machine, three agents "The same machine, rebuilt like a Swiss clock."

The five hardest questions

These can come at any point. Short answers, always landing on risk, cost or accountability.

"What if the AI makes a mistake?"
"It will sometimes flag things that are fine. That is by design: a specialist reviews every flag and rejects the wrong ones, and every rejection makes the system better. What it cannot do is act on a mistake, because it never changes a record and never makes a decision. Compare that with today, where 75% of cases are not looked at by anyone."
"Why not wait until the technology matures?"
"The technology is already in production in neighbouring regulated fields like pharmacovigilance, and regulators themselves use it. Meanwhile every month of waiting is a month at 25% coverage. Stage 0 costs 75 thousand and answers the maturity question with Roche's own data instead of opinions."
"Why you and not a big system integrator?"
"Three reasons. Our specialists already do this exact work for Roche today, so we start with the knowledge others would need months to acquire. We only invoice against proof at signed gates. And integrators quote two to eight million for implementation alone. Our model is fixed-price stages, zero licence fees, and no lock-in."
"Will the auditors and regulators accept an AI in this process?"
"The design was made for them: the AI only prepares, a person decides, every flag has written reasoning with sources, the model version is frozen and only changes through formal change control, and Stage 3 delivers a full validation package. Auditors get more traceability than today, not less."
"What happens if we stop, or if Redsparq disappears?"
"You can stop at any gate and pay nothing further. The source code is in escrow, documentation is a contractual deliverable, there is a 90-day handover commitment. There is no lock-in by design."

Words that may come up

Plain-language versions, in case someone asks what a term on the slides means.

PRI
Potential Reportable Incident. A customer case that might legally have to be reported to health authorities. Missing one is the big risk.
AI agent
A piece of AI software with one specific job, like reading cases or drafting a form. An assistant, not a decision-maker.
Claude
The AI engine we use, made by Anthropic. One of the leading AI models, particularly strong at following written rules and explaining itself.
Vertex AI
The Google Cloud service that runs the AI inside Roche's own cloud environment. It is how the model comes to the data instead of the data leaving.
Golden dataset
A set of real past cases whose correct answers are already known. The measuring stick used to test the AI at every gate.
GxP / validation
The quality rules for computer systems in the medical world. In practice: everything documented, tested, frozen, and changed only through a formal process.
Rexis & Salesforce
The two systems where customer cases are logged today. Part of the problem is that they follow two different quality standards.
Smartiva
The current 50-question validation form used for Salesforce cases only. Rigid: every question, every time, and nothing for Rexis.
MSA
The Master Service Agreement already in place between Roche and Redsparq. It is why Stage 0 can start without new contracting.
Stage gate
A checkpoint between project stages. Measured results are reviewed and signed off before the next stage, and its invoice, can start.
Escrow
The source code is deposited with a neutral third party. If anything happens to Redsparq, Roche gets the code.
TCO
Total cost of ownership. Everything over five years: build, operations, compute. Here: 4.3 to 5.2 million euros, with zero licence fees.
