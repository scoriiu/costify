## Slide 1 — Title

# vlocity

## Slide 2 — The Objective

# Validated guidance, exactly where agents work.

Customer Care agents follow picture-based, step-by-step workflows — which questions to ask, how to recognise a complaint or a PRI, what to record — **directly inside the Salesforce case.**

| Number | What it means |
|---|---|
| **~25** | Physical products covered in Phase 1 — digital products priced separately later |
| **~600** | Users on the platform Roche already runs (Salesforce / OmniStudio) |
| **€0** | New software needed to start — configure the existing platform |

*Every workflow is validated by subject-matter experts before release, with version history and a full audit trail.*

---

## Slide 3 — The Build

# Configured, not custom-coded.

### OmniScripts
Step-by-step guided workflows. The agent answers, irrelevant steps disappear, the right instruction and pictures appear.

### FlexCards
Product cards showing the agent, at a glance, the exact product with its workflows, manuals and pictures.

### Product catalog (EPC)
Images and document links attached to the product record — the right visuals follow the product automatically.

### File upload & mapping
Photos uploaded inside the workflow, linked automatically to the correct case and product records.

### CDN image hosting
High-resolution pictures on an external CDN, avoiding extra platform storage fees.

### Design once, repeat 25×
The consultant builds the templates; editors replicate them across the catalog. Product #26 takes days, not a development project.

## Slide 5 — The Logic Behind the Budget

# Designed once. Priced on evidence.

1. **Specialist designs, editors replicate.** One senior consultant builds the data model and templates; two lower-cost editors do the high-volume work across the catalog.
2. **The range is defined variables.** Formal GxP validation +€20–35K · master-data cleanup +€10–20K · more than ~12 workflows per product +€2–4K each. Not uncertainty — listed drivers.
3. **The pilot converts range into a firm quote.** One product built end-to-end in weeks 4–8 calibrates workflow count, data quality and validation depth. Then the price is fixed.
4. **Clean data is the biggest cost driver.** A one-week data-readiness check runs at project start; material cleanup is scoped via change control. Plan with the upper bound.

## Slide 6 — Licenses: The Biggest Variable

# Four scenarios. One phone call decides.

| Scenario | Cost | When it applies |
|---|---|---|
| S1 · Native platform licenses | ≈ €0 / year | Existing entitlements already cover the need — guided flows on licenses agents already hold |
| S2 · OmniStudio add-on — **most likely** | Low-to-mid six figures / year | Per-user add-on quoted by the Account Executive through the existing Salesforce agreement |
| S3 · Full Industry Cloud seats | ≈ €1.1–1.5M / year | Only if a platform upgrade is planned anyway — a platform decision, not a workflow decision |
| S4 · Separate third-party tool | ≈ €90–140K / year + setup | Only if "velocity" turns out to be a standalone product outside Salesforce |

*One conversation with the Salesforce Account Executive settles it — this single answer moves the license line by up to two orders of magnitude. If a paid scenario applies: start near 500 seats, expand mid-contract.*

## Slide 9 — Value & Success Metrics

# Quality of capture, not fewer cases.

### Handling time ↓
Agents follow the validated workflow instead of searching manuals — measurable reduction per case type.

### Capture rate ↑
Complaints and PRIs correctly identified first time at intake — the same quality objective App 2 verifies downstream.

### First-time-right data
Complete, consistent case fields — less downstream correction effort.

### Time-to-competence ↓
New agents reach proficiency faster when validated visual workflows replace tribal knowledge.

**Deliberately not a target: ticket deflection.** In regulated complaint intake, discouraging contact risks losing reportable events. Together with App 2, this closes one quality loop: prevent at intake, detect in review.
